From **8–12 June 2026**, the **24th Power Systems Computation Conference ([PSCC 2026](https://pscc2026.cy))** took over the **St. Raphael Resort in Limassol** for a week of computation, optimisation, dynamics, and grids by the sea. This was the **first PSCC ever hosted in Cyprus, and the first in the Eastern Mediterranean**: a milestone for the local power-systems community and for the conference.

PSCC 2026 was **jointly hosted by the two public universities of Cyprus**, the [University of Cyprus (UCY)](https://www.ucy.ac.cy/) in Nicosia and the [Cyprus University of Technology (CUT)](https://www.cut.ac.cy/) in Limassol, with our lab director {{% mention "p.-aristidou" %}} and Prof. Mathaios Panteli (UCY) serving as **Local Organising Committee co-chairs**. Members of the SPS Lab and our colleagues at EAC and CUT formed the backbone of the LOC.

## A truly international gathering

The numbers from the opening session tell the story:

- **358 registrations** as of 07/06/2026
- **34 countries** represented
- **40 parallel sessions** across four parallel tracks
- **35% student registrations**, the next generation of power systems researchers

The top countries by attendance were the **USA (11.5%)**, **Germany (9.5%)**, the **UK (8.4%)**, **France / Switzerland (7.0% each)**, and **Cyprus (6.7%)**, followed by Belgium, Spain, the Netherlands, Australia, Brazil, China, and Canada.

## A programme by and for the community

The week followed the classic PSCC structure, with a few Cyprus-flavoured additions:

- **Monday tutorial** on *Modelling, Control, Stability & Simulation of Low-Inertia Power Systems*, delivered by X. Guillaud, M. Paolone, F. Cecati, F. Milano and J. Vorwerk.
- **Monday round table** *"AI for Power, Power for AI"*, opening the conversation that would bookend the week.
- **Three plenary survey papers** on *TSO-DSO coordination for flexibility management*, *fundamentals of power system resilience*, and the closing **panel** on *Emerging risks of major supply-interruption events*.
- **40 parallel sessions** covering OPF, markets, protection, EMT, ML for power systems, IBR-dominated grids, state estimation, planning, physics-informed ML, storage, reliability, local markets, voltage and DER control, forecasting, TSO-DSO, HVDC, cyber-security, and frequency.

The social programme matched the science: a Welcome Cocktail at the venue on Tuesday, a guided **Mid-Conference Excursion** through Limassol's old town on Wednesday, and the **Gala Dinner** at the [Dafermou Winery](https://dafermouwinery.com/) in the Lefkara hills on Thursday, with a keynote dinner speech by **Frank Haeger (Gurobi)**.
